The Self-Built Classroom

Curiosity
over curriculum.

I grew up in a system that measured success through memorization. Curiosity was not rewarded; deviation was discouraged. For a long time, I felt my real abilities existed entirely outside the classroom.

“So I built my own classroom. Failures taught me to isolate variables, document results, and begin again.”

Whether experimenting with jackfruit seeds at home or designing magnetic safety systems for railway platforms — my path has been defined by the resolve to follow purpose with courage, even without certainty or applause.
