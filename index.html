Identity
The Self-Built Classroom

Curiosity
over curriculum.

I grew up in a system that measured success through memorization. Curiosity was not rewarded; deviation was discouraged. For a long time, I felt my real abilities existed entirely outside the classroom.

“So I built my own classroom. Failures taught me to isolate variables, document results, and begin again.”

Whether experimenting with jackfruit seeds at home or designing magnetic safety systems for railway platforms — my path has been defined by the resolve to follow purpose with courage, even without certainty or applause.

Team Project — 2nd Prize, Sub-district

Fire Fighter Rocker Boogie

Co-creator & Engineer

📷

A team-built firefighting and rescue robot designed for extreme terrain. The Rocker Boogie mechanism — inspired by Mars rover engineering — allows the robot to navigate smoothly across rubble, rocky mountain paths, and uneven disaster zones where conventional wheels would fail.

🔹 Key Capabilities
Equipped with automatic fire detection sensors that locate a fire source and trigger the extinguishing system autonomously. Built to assist firefighters in rescue missions in environments too dangerous or inaccessible for humans.
